Product Manager Interview Question Types

Each question type is designed to test different components of the essential skills every product manager needs. Your performance on these questions, not only directly effects the hiring decision but also the compensation package. Career Amplifier can help you navigate these questions.

Improve A Product

One of the most common product manager questions asked is 'pick a product that you like, and name one improvement'. It's a deceptively simple question that you are fully in control of. Frameworks and how you structure your response matters. A lack of preparation is noticeable, but the good news is that you can reuse your idea in future interviews.

Product Design

This is the traditional product manager question where you are asked to design a refrigerator for a blind person. It requires a general understanding of technology to do well, and it is intended to assess your organization, framework, and prioritization skills.

Launch A Product

For this question, the interviewer has you imagine that you're the PM in charge of a product. What do you launch next? You'll want to use a similar framework to how you practice with Product Design questions. This one can be difficult since the interviewer likely asks this question of every candidate and is able to quickly identify if your solution is strong or not.

Metrics & Data

There's often an assessment of your analytical skills. In the Facebook product manager interview, it's called 'Execution'. The interviewer provides a scenario where a metric has increased or decreased and you need to decide what to do next. Being able to imagine different possibilities requires a solid understanding of how the product works so that you can identify various options as you narrow down your answer.

Estimation

Google product manager interviews often include an estimation question. While this is not a question type that you should prepare for heavily, it's one you should practice at least once prior to a Google PM interview.

Recommended Books

There are a couple books that Career Amplifier recommends to help you with your interview prep.

Cracking the PM InterviewGayle Laakmann McDowell | Jackie Bavaro
Best PM Primer & Best Product Question Breakdown

This is the first book we recommend to someone looking to start their product manager career. McDowell and Bavaro have experience at some of the largest tech companies, and they detail how the product role is slightly different at each company. The last third of the book has what we consider to be the most in-depth breakdown of the product manager interview. This is the best book if you're just starting your product journey or if you need a refresher on the interview structure and product manager frameworks commonly used.
Decode and ConquerLewis Lin
Best Interview Dialogue Examples

Decode and Conquer is one of the most commonly referenced books when preparing for your product manager interview. It's completely focused on the product questions with less background on the PM role, and it does an amazing job of capturing dialogues for how to approach and frame your response. When you're first beginning on your journey, the product manager questions may seem simple and possibly too easy, and Lin allows you to get a better glimpse of what is possible.
